# Open Banking and Personal Financial Management: An SBI Investor Study

## Overview

This project is my MBA Finance research study on the impact of open banking initiatives on personal financial management among investors of State Bank of India (SBI) in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh.

As digital banking continues to evolve, open banking has started changing how individuals access financial services, manage investments, track spending, and make financial decisions. Through this project, I explored how these developments are influencing investor behaviour and financial management practices.

---

## Research Objective

The purpose of this study was to understand:

* How open banking initiatives are influencing personal financial management.
* The role of digital banking platforms in investment and financial decision-making.
* Adoption trends among SBI customers and investors.
* Key challenges limiting the adoption of open banking services.
* Opportunities for banks, fintech companies, and policymakers.

---

## What I Analyzed

### Open Banking Ecosystem

* Evolution of open banking globally and in India.
* Regulatory developments supporting digital financial services.
* Integration of banking and fintech platforms.

### Investor Behaviour

* Digital adoption trends among SBI customers.
* Use of digital channels for savings and investment products.
* Changes in budgeting, financial planning, and investment management.

### Challenges and Barriers

* Data privacy concerns.
* Trust and security issues.
* Financial literacy gaps.
* Adoption barriers among different user groups.

### Strategic Recommendations

Based on the findings, I developed recommendations for:

* State Bank of India
* Fintech partners
* Regulators
* Policymakers

to improve adoption, accessibility, and user confidence in open banking systems.

---

## Key Outcome

This project helped me understand how technology, regulation, and customer behaviour interact within the financial system.

Beyond studying banking products, the research focused on a broader question:

How can digital financial infrastructure help individuals make better financial decisions while maintaining trust, security, and accessibility?
